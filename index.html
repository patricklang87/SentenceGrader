Sentence Assesser

About

This automatic sentence scorer was developed with short-answer questions on language tests in mind. Many of the tests given, for example in German or Russian, provide sentence components and require that a sentence be constructed to demonstrate mastery of a simple grammar point in the context of an entire sentence. At UNC, our online teaching tool, Sakai, has several auto-grade components, including multiple choice, matching, and fill-in-the-blank. However, the automatic grader can only assess whether or not a user submission answers the answer key exactly or not. Therefore, when partial credit is given, instructors must grade exercises manually. This is an attempt to approximate a human grader's editing through automation.

Your Question

Next, you can can change the question value and weigh the various correction categories. The number of errors in each category will be multiplied by the given weight, and then subtracted from the question value in order to score the queston.









Now you can optionally choose a word you would like to give special weighting to. This might pertain to the specific grammar point your question is testing. Point deductions for errors made on this word will be multiplied by the selected weight factor. Note: This should be a word you expect to see in the answer.





Finally, provide an answer to test out your question.
